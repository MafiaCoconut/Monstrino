# Why Monstrino

Finding complete, reliable information about a collectible release
shouldn't require visiting six different websites and manually
comparing inconsistent data.

Yet for collectors of franchises like Monster High, that's the
reality today.

Product information is scattered across wikis, marketplaces, retailer
pages, and community blogs — each holding a different piece of the
puzzle, none of it structured or connected.

**Monstrino exists to fix that.**

---

## What You'll Learn in This Section

This section covers the reasoning behind Monstrino — from the
real-world problems it addresses, to the nature of catalog data,
to the platform architecture that makes it work.

### [Problem Space →](./01-problem-space)

Five structural problems that exist in the collector ecosystem today:
fragmented sources, unstructured data, missing package contents, no
pricing aggregation, and no franchise knowledge graph.

### [Catalog Data Reality →](./catalog-data-reality)

Why building a reliable catalog is a hard engineering problem.
Collectible data is distributed, inconsistent, incomplete, and
constantly evolving — which makes manual curation impossible to scale.

### [Monstrino Approach →](./monstrino-approach)

How Monstrino addresses these challenges: an automated ingestion
pipeline that collects, parses, enriches, and normalizes data from
multiple sources into a single canonical catalog.
